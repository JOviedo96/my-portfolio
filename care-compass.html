Overview

CareCompass is a patient-centered healthcare navigation platform designed to address systemic barriers identified through large-scale research. As Product Design Lead for my Master's capstone under Dr. Sang-Hwan Kim's mentorship, I led product strategy and design for a mobile platform that integrates with Electronic Medical Record systems via FHIR-compliant APIs.

Research Scale

As part of a 3-person research team, we analyzed 344,000+ patient app reviews and conducted 18 in-depth interviews to identify critical pain points: financial transparency, medical jargon barriers, and care coordination gaps.

This research is currently under review for publication in the International Journal of Medical Informatics, JMIR Human Factors, and BMC Medical Informatics and Decision Making.

Research & Discovery

Large-Scale Analysis + Deep Qualitative Research

I combined quantitative analysis at scale with deep qualitative investigation to understand patient navigation challenges:

  • 344,000+ Patient Reviews: Analyzed as part of research team to identify systemic patterns
  • 18 In-Depth Interviews: Conducted interviews with patients navigating chronic conditions
  • FHIR API Research: Investigated technical constraints of EMR integration

Key Insights

  • Financial Opacity: Patients struggle to understand costs before receiving care
  • Medical Jargon: Complex terminology creates comprehension gaps
  • Fragmented Care: Disconnected systems force patients to act as their own case managers

Product Design Strategy

FHIR-Compliant Integration Architecture

Rather than creating another disconnected platform, I designed for integration with existing EMR systems through FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources) APIs, ensuring CareCompass works within current healthcare infrastructure.

Core Features

  • Financial Transparency Module: Real-time cost estimates based on insurance coverage
  • Plain-Language Translation: Medical jargon automatically simplified with contextual definitions
  • Unified Care Timeline: Consolidated view across multiple providers
  • Smart Coordination: Automated reminders and care team communication

Key Takeaways

1

Research at Scale Reveals Systemic Patterns

Analyzing 344,000+ data points uncovered patterns invisible in small samples. Combining quantitative scale with qualitative depth creates complete understanding.

2

Design Within Constraints

Healthcare infrastructure is complex and regulated. Successful design means working within existing systems like FHIR standards rather than proposing unrealistic replacement.

3

Academic Rigor Strengthens Product Design

Applying research methodology to product work yields insights that withstand peer review, translating directly to more defensible design decisions.
