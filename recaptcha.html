Overview

This accessibility-focused redesign project applied rigorous research methodology to improve reCAPTCHA usability for users across vision ability spectrum. Through controlled A/B testing with 18 participants (normal vision, low vision, and no vision users), I designed and validated improvements that achieved statistically significant reductions in task completion time and error rates.

Research Rigor

Applied statistical analysis (p<0.001) to validate design improvements, demonstrating measurable impact on accessibility metrics. This project showcased expertise in both inclusive design principles and quantitative research methodology.

Research Methodology

Controlled A/B Testing

Designed rigorous testing protocol comparing original reCAPTCHA interface with accessibility-enhanced redesign across three user groups:

  • Normal Vision Users: Baseline group to ensure redesign didn't negatively impact standard experience
  • Low Vision Users: Users with reduced visual acuity testing contrast and sizing improvements
  • No Vision Users: Screen reader users testing alternative interaction patterns

Quantitative Metrics

Measured objective performance data:

  • Task completion time
  • Error rates
  • Abandonment rates
  • Retry attempts

Applied statistical analysis to validate that improvements were significant (p<0.001) rather than due to chance.

Design Solutions

Accessibility-Centered Redesign

Key design improvements focused on WCAG 2.1 AA compliance and inclusive interaction patterns:

  • Enhanced Contrast Ratios: Improved visual clarity for low vision users
  • Screen Reader Optimization: Clear semantic structure and ARIA labels
  • Keyboard Navigation: Fully keyboard-accessible interaction patterns
  • Alternative Challenges: Multiple verification options accommodating diverse abilities
  • Error Recovery: Clear error messaging and retry mechanisms

Key Takeaways

1

Quantitative Validation of Accessibility

Accessibility improvements must be measured and validated, not just asserted. Statistical rigor demonstrates real impact beyond anecdotal feedback.

2

Design Across Vision Ability Spectrum

Testing across normal, low, and no vision users ensures solutions work for diverse needs. Accessibility isn't binary—it's a spectrum requiring nuanced understanding.

3

Research Methodology Strengthens Design

Applying academic research rigor (controlled testing, statistical analysis) to design work produces defensible, evidence-based solutions that withstand scrutiny.
